Assignment 4

How do var, let, and const differ in terms of scope and hoisting? Write a code differentiating them.

The difference between var, let, and const in terms of scope and hoisting are as follows:

Scope:

  • var is function-scoped or globally-scoped. It does not have block scope.
  • let and const are block-scoped, meaning they are only accessible within the block they are defined.

Hoisting:

  • var declarations are hoisted to the top of their scope and initialized with undefined.
  • let and const are hoisted to the top of their block but are not initialized. Accessing them before declaration results in a ReferenceError.

Reassignment:

  • var can be re-assigned and re-declared.
  • const cannot be re-assigned or re-declared.
